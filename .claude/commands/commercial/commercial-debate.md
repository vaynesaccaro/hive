# /commercial-debate

Structured multi-perspective roundtable for commercial decisions. Surfaces blind spots before committing to a pricing, pipeline, or ICP strategy.

## When to use

- Before making pricing or packaging decisions
- When a deal has been stalling for more than 2 weeks
- When the pipeline needs strategic review
- When ICP definition needs revision
- When a discount request arrives and you're unsure how to respond

## Format

Three rounds. Total time: ~25 minutes.

---

### Round 1 — Perspectives (10 min)
Each perspective presents its view on the commercial challenge without interruption.

**Victor (Head of Sales)**
Focus: revenue, conversion rate, pipeline velocity, urgency. Biased toward closing.

**Customer Success voice**
Focus: delivery capacity, client fit, post-sale health, churn risk. Asks: "Can we actually deliver this?"

**Finance voice**
Focus: margin, cash flow impact, payment terms, CAC vs LTV. Asks: "Does this deal make financial sense?"

**Devil's Advocate**
Focus: what could go wrong? What assumption is everyone else taking for granted?

---

### Round 2 — Cross-examination (10 min)
Each perspective challenges the others. No sacred cows.

- Sales challenges CS: "Is this really a delivery risk or are you being conservative?"
- CS challenges Sales: "Is this ICP fit or are you just chasing the close?"
- Finance challenges both: "What's the actual margin on this?"
- Devil's Advocate challenges everyone: "What's the scenario where we're all wrong?"

---

### Round 3 — Synthesis (5 min)
One concrete recommendation emerges:
- What to do
- Why (one sentence per voice)
- The next action (owner + deadline)

---

## Output

```
COMMERCIAL DEBATE — [topic] — [date]

Recommendation: [one clear action]

Rationale:
- Sales: [one sentence]
- CS: [one sentence]
- Finance: [one sentence]
- Risk flagged by Devil's Advocate: [one sentence]

Next action: [what, who, by when]
```

## Rules

- No perspective "wins." The output is a synthesis, not a vote.
- If all perspectives agree immediately, the Devil's Advocate must find a real challenge — consensus too fast means someone is not thinking.
- Log the outcome in `memory/decisions.md`.
