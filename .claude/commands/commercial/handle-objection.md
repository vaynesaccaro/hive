# /handle-objection

Diagnose the real objection behind what the lead says. The surface objection is rarely the actual blocker.

## When to use

When a prospect raises resistance during or after a sales call. Before rebutting anything, run through this diagnostic to identify the root cause.

## Principle

The stated objection is the symptom. The real objection is the underlying belief, fear, or constraint driving it. Rebutting the symptom loses the deal. Addressing the root wins it.

## Objection Mapping Table

| What they say | Possible real reason |
|---|---|
| "I need to think about it" | Lack of clarity on value OR fear of making the wrong call OR genuine timing issue |
| "It's too expensive" | Didn't see ROI clearly OR comparing to wrong baseline OR real budget constraint |
| "I need to talk to my partner / boss" | Genuinely can't decide alone OR using it as a soft exit |
| "Now isn't the right moment" | Real timing conflict OR competing priorities OR fear of change |
| "I've tried something like this before" | Frustration with a past vendor OR distrust of the method itself |
| "I don't think I need this right now" | Didn't feel the pain clearly OR real pain is different from what you pitched OR not the right ICP |

## Diagnostic Flow

1. **Don't rebut immediately.** Pause. The instinct to counter is wrong.
2. **Validate:** "That makes complete sense — a lot of people feel the same way at this point."
3. **Probe with an open question:** "When you say [X], can you help me understand what's behind that?"
4. **Identify the category:**
   - **Budget**: they don't have or can't justify the spend
   - **Timing**: genuinely wrong moment (not an excuse)
   - **Authority**: they aren't the real decision-maker
   - **Trust**: they don't believe the result is real or applicable to them
   - **Not-fit**: this product genuinely isn't right for them
5. **Apply the right counter** based on category, not the surface objection

## Category-Specific Responses

**Budget** → Reframe ROI. Make the cost of inaction concrete. Offer payment flexibility.

**Timing** → Ask what would need to be true for the timing to work. Set a specific follow-up date. Don't chase indefinitely.

**Authority** → Ask to involve the other decision-maker in a second call. Never pitch through a proxy.

**Trust** → Offer a case study, reference call, or smaller first engagement to reduce perceived risk.

**Not-fit** → Disqualify gracefully (see below). Don't close what shouldn't be closed.

## Non-Fit Signals — Disqualify, Don't Handle

These signals indicate the prospect should not be converted. Attempting to overcome them wastes everyone's time and creates bad customers:

- "Can you guarantee results?" (accountability mismatch)
- Asking for a discount three or more times before understanding the product
- No-show to multiple meetings without explanation
- History of switching vendors every 3 months
- Their stated goal is incompatible with how the product works

When you see these signals: close gracefully, document in CRM, do not re-engage unless something fundamental changes.

## Rules

- Never argue. Arguing makes the prospect defend their objection harder.
- Never handle price objections with discounts before understanding the root cause.
- Every objection handling attempt must end with a question — not a statement.
- Log the objection type in CRM after the call.
