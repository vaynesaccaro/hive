# /marketing-debate

Marketing decision roundtable. Forces campaign, channel, and messaging decisions through multiple critical lenses before spending budget or publishing content.

## When to use

- Before launching a new campaign (paid or organic)
- When repositioning or updating core messaging
- When a channel is consistently underperforming
- When debating content strategy or editorial calendar shifts
- Before committing to a new content format or platform

## Format

Three rounds. Total time: ~25 minutes.

---

### Round 1 — Perspectives (10 min)
Each perspective presents its view on the marketing decision.

**Marketing Lead (Maya)**
Focus: brand coherence, reach, engagement, content quality, channel fit. Asks: "Does this strengthen or dilute the brand?"

**Sales voice**
Focus: lead quality from this channel, ICP alignment, how this affects pipeline. Asks: "Does this bring in the right people or just traffic?"

**Product voice**
Focus: whether the messaging accurately represents the product, positioning alignment, roadmap fit.

**CS voice**
Focus: the language real customers use vs the language marketing uses. Asks: "Is this how our customers actually describe their problem?"

**Finance voice**
Focus: CAC from this channel, budget ROI, cost per qualified lead, payback period.

**Devil's Advocate**
Focus: the contrarian view. What's the strongest case against this decision?

---

### Round 2 — Cross-examination (10 min)
Each perspective challenges the others directly.

- Marketing challenges Sales: "Are you asking for leads or for quality? Because those require different content."
- Sales challenges Marketing: "Is this content building brand or converting pipeline?"
- Product challenges Marketing: "Is the promise in this campaign deliverable by the actual product?"
- CS challenges Marketing: "Is this speaking to our customers' real language or the language we wish they used?"
- Finance challenges Marketing: "What's the cost per qualified lead, not just cost per click?"
- Devil's Advocate challenges everyone: "What's the scenario where this campaign backfires?"

---

### Round 3 — Synthesis (5 min)
Marketing Lead synthesizes the recommendation:
- Proceed / Proceed with modifications / Halt
- Key modifications if applicable
- Success metrics (what does "working" look like, and by when?)
- Next action: owner + deadline

---

## Output

```
MARKETING DEBATE — [topic] — [date]

Decision: [Proceed / Proceed with modifications / Halt]

Rationale:
- Marketing: [one sentence]
- Sales: [one sentence]
- Product: [one sentence]
- CS: [one sentence]
- Finance: [one sentence]
- Risk flagged by Devil's Advocate: [one sentence]

Modifications: [if applicable]
Success metrics: [what, measured how, by when]
Next action: [what, who, by when]
```

## Rules

- Budget decisions require Finance sign-off before the debate ends.
- If CS and Marketing language do not align, the CS language wins. Customers recognize their own words.
- Log the outcome in `memory/decisions.md`.
