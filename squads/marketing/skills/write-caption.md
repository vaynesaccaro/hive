# /write-caption

Write an Instagram or social media caption optimized for the specific post type and platform.

## When to use

When producing captions for Instagram carousels, Reels, single photos, or equivalent formats on other visual-first platforms. Run `/lookup-brand` first unless brand context is already loaded.

## Inputs

- `post_type`: `carousel` / `reel` / `photo`
- `topic`: what the post is about
- `tone`: on-brand tone for this specific post (e.g., direct, curious, challenging, story-driven)
- `cta_type`: `save` / `comment` / `dm` / `link_in_bio` / `share`

## Format

**Line 1: Hook (≤ 10 words)**
Stop the scroll. This line appears before "more" — it must earn the tap. No intros, no context, no brand name. Start with the tension.

**Lines 2–4: Expansion (2–3 short sentences)**
Build on the hook. Give just enough to make the reader want the full post. Do not over-explain — that's what the carousel or reel is for.

[blank line]

**CTA (1–2 sentences)**
One action only. Make it specific and low-friction.
- Save: "Save this for the next time you're stuck on [X]."
- Comment: "Drop [specific word/number] in the comments if this resonates."
- DM: "DM me [specific word] and I'll send you [specific thing]."
- Link in bio: "Full breakdown in the link in bio."
- Share: "Share this with someone who needs to hear it."

[blank line]

**Hashtags (5–8)**
Mix of broad (reach, discovery) and niche (community, ICP-specific). No more than 8 — beyond that, it reads as spam.

---

## Length guidelines

| Post type | Body length |
|---|---|
| Carousel | 150–300 words |
| Reel | 80–150 words |
| Single photo | 80–150 words |

---

## Rules

- **Line 1 must work in isolation.** It will be truncated in most feeds. If it doesn't stop the scroll on its own, rewrite it.
- **One CTA per caption.** Never stack "save this AND comment AND DM me." Pick one.
- **No hollow openers:** "In today's post...", "This one is important...", "Excited to share..." — all cut.
- **Emojis**: use sparingly and only if on-brand. Never use emojis as line-break decoration.
- **Hashtags at the end**, always. Mid-caption hashtags disrupt reading flow.
