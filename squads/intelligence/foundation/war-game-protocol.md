# War Game Protocol

A war game stress-tests a strategy or decision by simulating how competitors, customers, or adversarial forces would respond. Not a debate — a structured simulation with assigned roles.

---

## When to Run
- Before launching a major product change or pricing shift
- When entering a new market or segment
- When a key competitor makes a significant move
- Quarterly strategic review

---

## Setup

**Facilitator:** ___  (should not play a team role — stays neutral)
**Date / Duration:** ___  (typical: 90–180 min)
**Scenario / Trigger:** _What strategic move are we stress-testing?_

```
Example: "We announce a 30% price increase next month. 
         What happens?"
```

---

## Roles

| Team | Role | Participants |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Blue Team** | Our company — defend and adapt | ___ |
| **Red Team** | Primary competitor(s) — attack and counter | ___ |
| **Green Team** | Customers — react from the buyer's perspective | ___ |
| **Black Team** _(optional)_ | Wild card — disruptors, regulators, press | ___ |

Each team has **15–20 min** to prepare their position independently before the simulation.

---

## Simulation Rounds

| Round | Duration | Activity |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 — Opening moves | 20 min | Blue announces strategy. Red + Green react. |
| 2 — Counter-moves | 20 min | Red escalates. Green shifts behavior. Blue adapts. |
| 3 — Escalation | 15 min | All teams push to their most aggressive position. |
| 4 — Debrief | 30 min | Facilitator leads structured review (see below). |

---

## Debrief Structure

1. **What surprised us?** (Facilitator asks each team)
2. **What is our biggest vulnerability exposed?**
3. **What assumptions turned out to be wrong?**
4. **Top 3 strategic adjustments we should consider?**

---

## Output

| Item | Owner | Due |
|------|-------|-----|
| War game summary doc | Facilitator | Within 48h |
| Strategic adjustment recommendations | Leadership | Within 1 week |
| Follow-up issues created | Relevant squad lead | Within 1 week |

---

## Rules
- Roles are played honestly — no soft-pedaling to protect feelings
- No "yes but" during simulation rounds — commit to the position
- All output is internal and confidential
