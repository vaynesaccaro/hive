# Churn Protocol

## Early Warning Signals
- Health score drops to Yellow (60–79) for 2+ consecutive weeks
- No login / product activity for **___ days**
- Open P1 support ticket unresolved > **3 business days**
- Stakeholder change (champion leaves, new buyer)
- Contract not renewed within **30 days** of renewal date
- Client explicitly mentions cancellation or competitor evaluation

---

## Intervention Triggers

| Signal | Trigger | Response |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Health score → Yellow | Immediate | CS proactive call within 48h |
| Health score → Red | Immediate | CS + CS Manager escalation call within 24h |
| No activity > ___ days | Automatic alert | Re-engagement sequence starts |
| Cancellation request | Immediate | Escalate to CS Lead + Account Executive |

---

## Escalation Path
1. **CS Rep** — first response, root cause discovery
2. **CS Manager** — if unresolved in 5 business days or Red score
3. **Account Executive** — if contract risk confirmed
4. **Exec Sponsor** — if ARR > $___  or strategic account

---

## Intervention Playbook

### Step 1 — Discovery Call
- Goal: identify root cause (product gap, support failure, internal change, budget)
- Do not offer discounts at this stage
- Document outcome in CRM

### Step 2 — Recovery Plan
- Agree on 2–3 specific actions with clear owners and dates
- Set follow-up call within **7 days**
- Send written summary to client

### Step 3 — Escalation (if no improvement)
- Exec-level call offered
- Custom support window or concession considered (with approval)

---

## Win-Back Playbook (Post-Churn)
- **30 days post-churn:** Check-in email — no pitch, just relationship
- **90 days post-churn:** Product update outreach if relevant improvement shipped
- **180 days post-churn:** Formal re-engagement with new offer if ICP still fits
- Disqualify win-back if: churned due to product mismatch or regulatory constraint

---

## Churn Logging
- Log churn reason in CRM: `price | product gap | champion left | no ROI | competitor | unknown`
- Monthly churn retrospective: top 3 reasons + action items
