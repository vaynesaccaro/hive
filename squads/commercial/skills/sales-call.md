# /sales-call

7-part structured sales call framework based on the Challenger Sale methodology. Guides a discovery-to-close conversation in under 90 minutes.

## When to use

Before any sales call where the goal is to move a prospect toward a decision. Especially useful when the salesperson is junior, the deal is complex, or the product requires context-building before price discussion.

## Steps / Framework

### Part 1 — Warm-up (2–3 min)
- Connect personally: find one genuine point of common ground
- Build trust: reference something specific you researched about them
- Identify the core pain in one open question: "What made you agree to this call today?"

### Part 2 — Reframing (10–15 min)
Present a personalized strategic diagnosis. For each problem they named, show:

| Problem | Real Impact | Our Approach | Transformation |
|---|---|---|---|
| [their problem] | [cost of this problem] | [your method] | [what changes] |

- Include one concrete result from a comparable client
- Show a relevant market comparison to anchor the stakes

### Part 3 — Controlled Urgency (10 min)
- Cost of inaction: what does staying the same cost per month/quarter?
- Competitor adoption: is this becoming standard in their space?
- Inaction vs Action table:

| If they do nothing | If they act now |
|---|---|
| [specific cost] | [specific gain] |

- Close this section with a relevant case study

### Part 4 — Emotional Impact (5–10 min)
- Introspection questions: "Where would you be in 12 months if this doesn't change?"
- One powerful analogy that makes the pain visceral
- Address the technology/change fear: "Most of our clients felt the same before starting"
- Name the real fear behind their hesitation

### Part 5 — New Path (10 min)
- Present practical solutions matched to their budget and operational maturity
- Show the first step concretely: "Here's what week one looks like"
- Make it feel achievable, not overwhelming

### Part 6 — The Solution (15–20 min)
- Personalized demo or walkthrough
- Present plans (e.g., Starter / Growth / Enterprise) with clear differences
- Show a 30-day implementation schedule
- Name 2–3 exclusive bonuses or onboarding supports

### Part 7 — Closing (10–15 min)
- Anticipate the top 5 objections (see `/handle-objection`)
- Price anchoring: establish perceived value first, then reveal real price
- Payment options: offer at least 2 (monthly, annual with discount)
- Closing question: "Based on what we discussed, which plan makes the most sense for where you are now?"
- Always ask for referrals before hanging up, regardless of outcome

## Rules

- **Never mention price before Part 7.** Price before value destroys the close.
- **Max 90 minutes total.** If it runs longer, something is off in Parts 2–4.
- **Never accept "I need to think" without understanding why.** Ask: "What specifically would make this clearer for you?"
- **Register outcome in CRM after the call:** stage, objection raised, follow-up date, decision timeline.
- **No pitch monologue.** Each part should include at least one question back to the prospect.
