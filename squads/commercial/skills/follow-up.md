# /follow-up

Structure the post-meeting follow-up sequence for a lead after a sales call or proposal.

## When to use

Immediately after any sales conversation ends — whether a demo, discovery call, or proposal delivery. Prevents deals from dying in the silence after a good call.

## Inputs

- `lead_name`: prospect name
- `meeting_date`: date of the last interaction
- `outcome`: one of — `closed` / `proposal_sent` / `second_meeting_scheduled` / `stalled`
- `main_objection`: the primary hesitation raised (optional but valuable)

## Steps / Framework

### Step 1 — Same-day message (within 2 hours of the call)
Send a brief message covering:
- One-line recap of what was discussed ("We covered X and agreed Y is the priority")
- The specific next step confirmed on the call ("You mentioned you'd review the proposal by Thursday")
- No hard pitch — just clarity and reliability

### Step 2 — If proposal was sent: D+2 follow-up
- Ask if they had a chance to review
- Offer to clarify one specific section (shows you read their hesitation)
- One question only: "Is there anything in the proposal that raised questions?"

### Step 3 — If stalled (no response after D+2): D+7 value-angle message
- Do not resend the proposal
- Bring new value: a relevant case study, a market insight, a short observation
- Frame it around them, not around the sale: "Saw this and thought of what you mentioned about [X]"

### Step 4 — If still no response: D+14 breakup message
Use the "closing your file" technique. Example structure:
- "I'm going to close out this conversation on my end — I don't want to keep sending messages that aren't useful to you."
- "If the timing changes, I'm easy to find."
- This creates low-pressure urgency without desperation. Response rate on breakup messages is often higher than standard follow-ups.

### Step 5 — After each touchpoint
- Log the interaction in CRM: date, message type, response (yes/no/partial)
- Move the lead to the appropriate pipeline stage
- If no response after D+14: move to long-term nurture list, not continued active follow-up

## Rules

- **One question per message.** Multiple questions get zero answers.
- **Never multiple CTAs in a single message.** Pick one.
- **Tone: direct, not pushy.** Never apologize for following up.
- **Never resend the proposal without being asked.** Resending signals desperation.
- **Never follow up more than 4 times without a response signal.** Move to nurture.
- **Always log in CRM** — follow-up that isn't logged didn't happen.
